# V16 — Timestamp Dependence

## What to check
1. Every use of `block.timestamp` for lock expiry, cooldowns, deadlines, or vesting cliffs.
2. For each use: assess the economic impact of ±15 seconds of miner/validator manipulation.
   - 15 seconds is irrelevant for a 1-year vesting cliff.
   - 15 seconds is material for a 30-second auction deadline.
3. Boundary operators — verify `<` vs `<=` around every time comparison. Off-by-one can allow early action or block timely action.

## L2 BLOCK.NUMBER SEMANTICS
If {chain} is Arbitrum, Optimism, Base, zkSync, or another L2:
- Verify no time-measurement logic relies on `block.number` to compute durations or deadlines.
- On Arbitrum: `block.number` increments per transaction, not per L1 block. An `endBlock - startBlock` window is severely compressed under normal load.
- Flag any such usage as Medium regardless of the estimated impact magnitude.

## Severity guidance
- `block.timestamp` used for high-value deadline where 15s manipulation enables profitable exploit: Medium or High depending on confirmed extractable value.
- `block.number` used for duration measurement on Arbitrum: Medium.
- Off-by-one in boundary operator enabling early unlock by 1 second: Low or Medium depending on context.
- Timestamp use where manipulation window has no economic consequence: Low or Info.
